## Viability Theory (HIGH PRIORITY)

Jean-Pierre Aubin's viability theory (1991). The set of states that CAN
remain admissible under constraints. Doesn't pull — just survives.

Need:

- Exact fit and mismatch with the system's constraint-on-distinguishability

This may be the closest formal external concept to what the system does.
Closer than "attractor basin" because viability is constraint-first,
not dynamics-first.

## Stochastic Thermodynamics

Seifert, Jarzynski, Crooks. Fluctuation theorems. Connects entropy
production to information in far-from-equilibrium systems. May formalize
the neg/pos feedback coupling.

## Autopoiesis (Maturana, Varela)

Self-producing systems. Organizational closure. May connect to the
self-grounding property (F01+N01 constraining the process itself).
