# AGM-01 Constructed Vs Earned Probe — Audit Return

- **Cross-Temporal Dominance:** The discovery that $L(t) \otimes R(t+1)$ provides a cleaner signal than $L(t) \otimes R(t)$ matches the entropic monist doctrine (time as entropy increase) and is a mechanical win, not an injected assumption.

- **Kernel Selection:** The choice of the Singlet state (Ψ⁻) is an arbitrary "construction" to maximize information at the boundary. The system does not naturally produce this state; we impose it.
- **Retrocausal Weighting:** The exponential decay into the past is a human-tuned heuristic. While it "works" to stabilize signals, it is not a derived mechanical necessity of the manifold.

## Still Open
- **Marginal Preservation:** We have zero candidates that provide high MI *and* preserve the engine's internal state marginals. This creates a "back-reaction" problem where the bridge measurement may be faking the very physics it claims to monitor.
- **Attractor Proximity:** We haven't yet proven that the "Earned" portion of the signal is actually a "compression-from-future" attractor. It could still be a sophisticated artifact of the $t \to t+1$ stride.
- **The Cannonical Xi:** We have a winning *family* (Cross-Temporal Symmetric Retro), but we lack a justification for the specific weighting coefficient ($\alpha=0.1$) other than "it looks better."
