## 1. Classical Matrix Dissolution
To develop the formal math for Irrational Game Theory (IGT) across all 16 engine stages, we must permanently strip away classical scalar payoff matrices. The "game" is defined entirely through the thermodynamic evolution of a shared density matrix $\rho$. 

The 4 primitive strategies (`WIN`, `win`, `LOSE`, `lose`) are mathematically defined by two variables:
1. **Direction** (The sign of the entropy gradient, measured via the Survivorship Functional $\Phi(\rho) = \ln(d) - S(\rho)$).
2. **Magnitude** (The coupling strength $\gamma$ distinguishing the Outer Major loop from the Inner Minor loop).

### Thermodynamic Definitions
*   **WIN (Maximax / Major Gain):** $\Delta\Phi \gg 0$. Executed on the Outer Loop with dominant coupling ($\gamma_{dom}$). Generates massive macroscopic structure but rapidly accumulates topological winding debt.
*   **win (Minimax / Minor Gain):** $\Delta\Phi > 0$. Executed on the Inner Loop with subordinate coupling ($\gamma_{sub}$). Tactical, low-yield structure gain.
*   **LOSE (Minimin / Major Sacrifice):** $\Delta\Phi \ll 0$. Executed on the Outer Loop with dominant coupling ($\gamma_{dom}$). Massive structural sacrifice; pays the Landauer erasure cost to actively reset global topological winding.
*   **lose (Maximin / Minor Cost):** $\Delta\Phi < 0$. Executed on the Inner Loop with subordinate coupling ($\gamma_{sub}$). Tactical maintenance cost; a small energy expenditure to maintain phase alignment.
