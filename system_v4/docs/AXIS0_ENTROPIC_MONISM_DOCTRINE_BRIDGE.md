## 1. The Misnamed Term: "Retrocausal" Weighting

The Phase 4 winning bridge (`cross_s1_symmetric_retro`, MI=1.539) uses exponential
time-decay weighting: `w(t) = exp(-0.1 * (T - 2 - t))`. This gives the LAST trajectory
pairs the highest weight and the earliest pairs the lowest.

This was labelled "retrocausal" because later states carry more weight. But in the
entropic monism framework, this is not backward causation — the past does not cause the
present in this model. The correct reading is:

**Later trajectory states carry more weight because they are closer to the compressed attractor.**

The engine trajectory converges toward its attractor basin. Later steps are more
compressed (lower entropy, higher structure) than earlier steps. The weighting correctly
up-weights the high-compression, high-information end of the trajectory.

Rename candidate: **external-compression weighting** or **attractor-proximity weighting**.
