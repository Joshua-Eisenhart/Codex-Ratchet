# Proto-Ratchet Rival Realization Candidates

**Date:** 2026-03-29  
**Status:** Strict proto-ratchet candidate menu for concrete rival realizations downstream of the root constraints. This file turns the broader branch-space packet into a more operational menu: what concrete non-Hopf realization candidates exist now, what layer they belong to, what would promote them, and what must not be compared at the wrong layer.

---

## 1. Purpose

This packet answers:

**If the current favored live basin is finite-QIT/Hopf/Weyl, what concrete rival realization candidates are still alive, and what evidence would be needed to promote any of them?**

The key rule is:

- do not compare pre-metric structural realizations against late metric overlays as if they were peers
- do not compare executable working packets against pure admissibility sketches as if they had equal evidential weight

## 3. Promotion Classes

These candidates do not all compete at the same level.

| Class | Meaning | Members |
|---|---|---|
| structural realizations | can serve as real pre-metric geometry realizations of the constraint ladder | compatibility-topology, distinction-separation, persistent-loop, bundle-like, relation/adjacency, refinement-order |
| path/organizational realizations | can organize admissible constructions without yet giving a full geometric packet | finite-path, restricted category-like |
| derived geometry lenses | require later admissions before they can compete | graph/correlation, information geometry, Hilbert-like / inner-product |
| favored executable basin | strongest current working realization packet | finite-QIT/Hopf/Weyl |

So the correct comparison rule is:

- compare structural realizations against structural realizations
- compare derived geometry lenses against other late lenses
- do not compare a late removable Hilbert-like overlay directly against the whole earned pre-Hopf ladder

## 5. Promotion Criteria

What would it take for a rival to challenge the current favored basin?

| Candidate class | Promotion criterion |
|---|---|
| structural realization | must support real axis slices `A_i : M(C) -> V_i` without borrowing the whole Hopf/QIT packet |
| path/organizational realization | must earn topology/loop/obstruction structure cleanly enough to host axes, not just path bookkeeping |
| derived geometry lens | must show it is more than a descriptive overlay and can reconstruct a stable owner packet |
| executable side-branch | must have live probes/sims and clean handoff from constraints, not just convenience code |

Strongest current challenge paths:

1. graph/correlation realization  
2. refinement-order realization  
3. persistent-loop / bundle realization without automatic Hopf closure

## 8. Do Not Smooth

- Do not treat “alive rival” as “co-equal owner packet.”
- Do not demote the current finite-QIT/Hopf/Weyl basin just because alternatives remain admissible.
- Do not promote late metric or Hilbert-like overlays into rival primitives.
- Do not let graph/correlation realizations skip the constraint-handoff burden.
- Do not let path realizations import time or cycles before those are earned.
