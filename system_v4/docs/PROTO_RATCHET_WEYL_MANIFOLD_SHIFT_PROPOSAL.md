**Purpose:** Explore a stronger geometric reading in which left/right Weyl spinors belong to the manifold-side ambient geometry, the engines are degrees of freedom running on that geometry, nested Hopf tori are an intermediate realization rather than the final outermost geometry, and `Axis 0` remains a later cut-state functional rather than a geometry synonym.

## 3. Proposed Shift

The proposed shift is not "throw out Hopf/Weyl." It is:

1. keep the current ratchet order
2. demote nested Hopf tori from "final geometry feeling" to "intermediate realization rung"
3. treat left/right Weyl spinors as ambient manifold-side structure outside the engines
4. treat engine axes as degrees of freedom on that ambient geometry
5. keep `Axis 0` as a later cut-state functional rather than a geometry label

Proposed reading:

```text
roots
-> M(C)
-> allowed finite QIT/probe/state math
-> deeper geometry ladder
-> S^3 / Hopf / Bloch realization
-> nested Hopf-tori realization
-> left/right Weyl ambient sheet structure
-> engine degrees of freedom on that geometry
-> bridge Xi : geometry/history -> rho_AB
-> Axis 0 kernel on cut states
```

Under this shift:

- `S^3`, `S^2`, Hopf, and nested Hopf tori are real and useful
- but they are not presumed to be the terminal outermost geometry
- left/right Weyl spinors are not engine labels first; they are ambient geometric structure first
- the engines become structured traversals or DOF on that Weyl-side geometry

This also fits the user proposal that:

- Bloch spheres, spinors, density matrices, and probes all belong in the real ladder
- each layer constrains the next layer more strongly
- the full system should be thought of as ratcheting geometric constraint manifolds, not one single flattened geometry statement

## 4. Consequences For Axes

If the Weyl-manifold shift is right, the axes should be re-read as follows.

### Ax0

- stays later than geometry
- stays later than the engines
- remains the strongest candidate for a cut-state conditional-entropy / coherent-information family
- should not be collapsed into `eta`, `sgn(r_z)`, or black/white alone

### Ax1

- remains likely derived and operational
- becomes even less plausible as primitive geometry
- may survive as a branch split induced by engine DOF on the manifold rather than as a deep ontological axis

### Ax2

- remains live, but becomes more suspect as a final interpretation
- direct/conjugated may be an engine-side representation DOF on Weyl geometry rather than the deepest manifold split

### Ax3

- remains operationally useful
- but may be better understood as an engine traversal class on the ambient geometry rather than a direct geometry primitive
- `fiber/base` still looks stronger than `inner/outer` as universal carrier language

### Ax4

- remains operationally useful as ordering / winding / traversal structure
- but becomes more suspect as a final axis binding if actual geometric orientation, chirality, or winding on the Weyl manifold turns out richer than the current `UEUE` / `EUEU` proxy

### Ax5

- stays an engine generator-family axis
- should be treated as dynamics on geometry, not geometry itself

### Ax6

- can remain a strong derived closure for the current engine
- but its current strength is structural/operational, not proof that the deeper geometry story is finished

## 6. What Becomes Suspect

The Weyl-manifold shift puts pressure on these current reads.

- treating nested Hopf tori as if they were the final outermost geometry
- treating the Weyl layer as merely a compiled working add-on instead of potentially ambient manifold-side structure
- treating `Axis 0` geometric seat at `eta` as too close to the final `Axis 0` object
- treating the current `b0` / `sgn(r_z)` boolean as if it were equivalent to the cut-state `Axis 0` kernel
- treating Ax3 and Ax4 as fully settled metaphysical bindings rather than strong current operational bindings
- treating Ax2 as fully final instead of a candidate engine-side representation DOF
- treating the current engine as if it already expresses the deepest geometry rather than one DOF system running on a deeper ladder

Most pressure points under this shift:

1. `Axis 0` seat versus `Axis 0` kernel
2. Weyl ambient structure versus engine-local axis structure
3. nested Hopf tori as intermediate rung versus final geometry
4. operationally locked axes versus deeper geometric meaning
